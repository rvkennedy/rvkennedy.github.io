1 4 Rendering the Environment {#man_4_rendering}
2 =========================
3 
4 Weather Rendering is handled by \link simul::clouds::BaseWeatherRenderer BaseWeatherRenderer\endlink, or (if necessary) a derived class of this.
5 
6 All Simul's default renderers use a right-handed co-ordinate system with x pointing East, y North, and z up.
7 
8 The Weather Renderer owns and manages renderers for sky, clouds, atmospherics and other effects.
9 So the Weather Renderer is the main interface between your program and trueSKY.
10 
11 The following table describes the elements of a sky as implemented in the supplied renderers:
12 
13 | Feature |
14 | ------------------------- |
15 | Cosmic Background |
16 | Stars |
17 | Planets |
18 | Volumetric Clouds |
19 | 2D Clouds |
20 | Mixed resolution rendering|
21 | Atmospherics |
22 | Godrays |
23 | Precipitation |
24 | Rain streaks |
25 
26 Cosmic Background
27 -----------------
28 The cosmic background texture is drawn first, at an orientation that corresponds to the plane of the Milky Way galaxy - see \link simul::sky::SkyKeyframer::SetBackgroundBrightness BackgroundBrightness\endlink.
29 
30 Stars
31 -----
32 Stars are drawn as points, and spun around the axis of the Earth's rotation, in the same direction as the sun. Stars are usually drawn to the highest-resolution buffer (i.e. the frame buffer or final buffer). See
33 \link simul::sky::SkyKeyframer::SetStarBrightness StarBrightness\endlink and \link simul::sky::SkyKeyframer::SetMaxStarMagnitude MaxStarMagnitude\endlink.
34 
35 The Sun
36 -------
37 The sun is drawn after the stars.
38 
39 Planets
40 -------
41 Planets (e.g. the Moon) are drawn by BaseSkyRenderer.
42 A planet texture map needs to be supplied, and the shader takes a sun direction as a parameter -
43 this is used to light the planet (e.g. phases of the moon). Like stars, planets are usually drawn to the
44 highest-resolution buffer, in order to make sure they have sharp outlines.
45 
46 
47 Sky/Atmospherics
48 ----------------
49 The sky and atmospherics are drawn to a volumetric texture for which the x and y axes are screen coordinates, and the z axis is distance. The final compositing step puts the sky, atmospherics and clouds to the current
50 rendertarget.
51 
52 Clouds
53 ------
54 Clouds are usually drawn to a pair of lower-resolution buffers.
55 The \link simul::clouds::BaseCloudRenderer BaseCloudRenderer\endlink keeps the cloud volume textures up to date, and draws the clouds volumetrically.
56 
57 Performance
58 -----------
59 Rendering performance is chiefly dependent on the number of pixels drawn, and the number of raycasting steps (or Cloud Slices).
60 Calculation performance - mainly cloud lighting - depends strongly on the grid size - the larger the grid, the more time will be taken to light a cloud keyframe.
61 
62 To adjust the number of drawn layers, the simul::clouds::CloudKeyframer class offers the maximum
63 distance (in kilometres) with
64 \link simul::clouds::CloudKeyframer::SetMaxCloudDistanceKm SetMaxCloudDistanceKm\endlink
65 and BaseCloudRenderer has the maximum number of layers (\link simul::clouds::BaseCloudRenderer::SetMaxSlices SetMaxSlices\endlink) functions.
66 
67 It is usual to downsample the cloud part of the sky rendering to 1/2 or 1/4 resolution. This is controlled by
68 \link simul::clouds::BaseWeatherRenderer::SetDownscale SetDownscale\endlink
69 in the \link simul::clouds::BaseWeatherRenderer weather renderer\endlink.
70 
71 
72 Rain and Snow
73 -------------
74 
75 \image html "rain.jpg" ""
76 
77 The \link simul::clouds::CloudKeyframe::precipitation precipitation\endlink is used to control the amount of rain or snowfall
78 at a given time, whilst the \link simul::clouds::CloudKeyframer PrecipitationThresholdKm\endlink property is used to set the thickness of cloud required for rain to fall (setting this to 0 will allow even the smallest of clouds to produce rain). To switch between snow and rain use \link simul::clouds::CloudKeyframe rain_to_snow\endlink (where 0 is rain, 1 is snow).
79 
80 Each cloud keyframe has a \link simul::clouds::PrecipitationRegion PrecipitationRegion\endlink, which defines the local area of precipitation,
81 if it is not global for the keyframe. The amount of particles used for precipitation can be altered with the \link simul::clouds::BasePrecipitationRenderer MaxParticles\endlink property.
82 
83 \image html "Lightning-RainStreaks.jpg" ""
84 
85 Classes derived from simul::clouds::BasePrecipitationRenderer are used to draw the rain or snow particles, while the \link simul::clouds::PrecipitationRegion:rainEffectStrength rain streak effect\endlink is drawn by the cloud renderer. This class has a property "UseSimulationTime" which determines whether rain and snow speed is based on the simulated time-of-day, or real time in seconds.
86
