56  /* http://www-das.uwyo.edu/~geerts/cwx/notes/chap09/hydrometeor.html
57  Rain falls at 6-7 m/s.
58  Snow falls at about 1 m/s, slightly faster aloft because of the density
59  correction [exp(z/20)].
60  Hail can fall much faster,its speed is approximately 1.4 D^0.8 (D is in mm)
61  at sea level, the exact relationship depends on hail density and shape.
62 
63  The surface area of a snowflake increases about as rapidly as its weight,
64  so the speed of snow increases only slightly with flake size : .
65  Fall speed relation for unrimed snow is (3):
66  V = a D^b (0.7 < a < 1.2, 0.11 < b < 0.16)
67  where V in m/s, D in mm
68  */
