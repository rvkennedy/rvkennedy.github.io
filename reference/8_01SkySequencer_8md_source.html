1 6 The Sky Sequencer {#man_8_sequencer}
2 =================
3 
4 The Sky Sequencer is a tool for artists, technical artists, level designers,
5  and mission planners. It also helps programmers to understand the workings of True Sky's sky,
6  clouds and time-of-day systems.</p>
7 
8 To launch the Sequencer, go to Start Menu/All Programs/Simul/Sky Sequencer/Sky Sequencer.
9 
10 The Sequencer Window
11 ====================
12 
13 The Timeline
14 -------------
15 \image html "Timeline.png"
16 
17 To change the current time, drag the red vertical line (the time marker) by its
18 handle. To change the visible time-window, drag the timeline at the bottom. To
19 adjust the range of visible time, use the mousewheel to zoom in and out.
20 
21 To select keyframes, either click on a single keyframe, shift-click to select
22 multiple keyframes, or box-select them. When one or more keyframes are selected,
23 their properties can be changed in the property box. Changing any property
24 affects all selected keyframes of the appropriate type (e.g. all cloud
25 keyframes, or all sky keyframes.)
26 
27 To select a whole row of keyframes, double-click on the space in-between them.
28 
29 To select a layer (the sky, a layer of clouds, etc.), click on its name on the left
30 of the timeline.
31 
32 To add a keyframe, right-click on the timeline and select the "Add sky
33 keyframe..." or "Add cloud keyframe..." option.
34 
35 Sky keyframes determine the colours of the sky from horizon to zenith, any haze
36 effect, and light colour.
37 
38 Cloud keyframes determine the properties of the cloud layer at a given time. As
39 the light direction changes with time of day, it is necessary to have enough
40 cloud keyframes to reflect the lighting throughout the day.
41 
42 Map Window
43 ----------
44 This window shows the cloud keyframes in a plan view. Wind heading and speed affects the
45 position of each keyframe - or they can be positioned individually. The camera is shown
46 as a red line indicating its direction of view. Rain regions are shown as filled grey circles
47 (blue when highlighted).
48 
49 Properties Window
50 -----------------
51 
52 The Properties Window can be activated from the Window>>Properties menu option. Whatever
53 is selected in the timeline can be edited in the properties window. Its contents depend
54 on the current selection.
55 
56 When nothing is selected (click on a blank part of the timeline), the general properties
57 are shown, including camera and view properties.
58 
59 Performance Window
60 ------------------
61 <table>
62 <tr>
63 <td>
64 The Performance Window can be activated from the Window>>Performance menu option. When
65 active, this window shows a text display of GPU and CPU profiling data for trueSKY. The GPU
66 data is taken from the DirectX11 view.
67 
68 The values are shown in a hierarchical tree - the time for each element includes the times
69 of its children. The numbers given are times in milliseconds. The colour of the text for
70 each item indicates what percentage of its parent time it occupies. The more red the text,
71 the larger its contribution.
72 
73 You'll see a slight pause in the Sequencer about once per second as the performance window
74 updates - this doesn't affect the measurements.
75 </td>

82 Features
83 ========
84 
85 <h2>Cloud features
86 </h2>
87 <h3>Grid</h3>
88 <p>The Grid height and width determine the amount of detail in a cloud volume.</p>
89 <h3>Cloud volume width</h3>
90 <p>The cloud volume may be typically 10-100km wide. It can repeat (wrap), so that it
91  covers the whole sky. Alternatively, a non-wrapping cloud volume can represent a
92  specific area of cloud in a clear sky.</p>
93 <h3>Keyframe features</h3>
94 The main properties for controlling the weather are:
95 
96 <table style="width: 718px; background-color: #E3EEFB; margin-left: 56px;">
97 <tr><th>Feature</th><th><b>Description</b></th></tr>
98 <tr><td>cloudiness</td><td>How much cloud in the layer at this time.</td></tr>
99 <tr><td>cloudbase (km)</td><td>Base altitude.</td></tr>
100 <tr><td>cloud height (km)</td><td>How tall the cloud layer is.</td></tr>
101 </table>
102 
103 See the public attributes of \link simul::clouds::CloudKeyframe CloudKeyframe\endlink for a full description of all the cloud keyframe properties.
104 
105 <h2>Sky features</h2>
106 
107 <h3>Brightness Power</h3>
108 The sky at sunrise can be less than one quarter as bright as at noon. Moonlight is hundreds of times dimmer
109 than sunlight. Starlight is much dimmer still. The human eye adjusts to a very wide range of light conditions, but for rendering we usually apply a brightness,
110 or exposure level, to make dark scenes visible and bright scenes clear.<br />
111 The Brightness Power value for a sky sequence adjusts the light levels to compensate for this large variation by introducing a power function.
112 If the Power is 1.0, there is no adjustment. If it is 0.5, it will be a square-root dependence, e.g. a light 4 times dimmer than daylight would
113 appear only half as dim, and so on.
114 
115 <h3>Automatic Sun Position</h3>
116 <p>The sun is automatically placed in the sky based on time-of-day if this is enabled. Otherwise, each sky keyframe has sun
117  position settings.</p>
118 <h3>Automatic Moon Position</h3>
119 <p>If enabled, the moon is automatically placed in the sky based on time, day,
120  month, and year. Otherwise, each sky keyframe has moon position settings.</p>
121 <h3>Colour Sky</h3>
122 <p>A colour sky has colours for sky and fade determined by a choice from a colour dialog. The default behaviour (non-colour sky) is to generate the colours from physical properties).
123 </p>
124 <h3>Keyframe features</h3>
125 See the class definition for \ref simul::sky::SkyKeyframe.
126 The main sky keyframe properties are:
127 
128 <table style="width: 718px; background-color: #E3EEFB; margin-left: 56px;">
129 <tr><th>Feature</th><th><b>Description</b></th></tr>
130 <tr><td>Haze</td><td>How much haze, or fog is present at this
131  time.</td></tr>
132 <tr><td>Sun azimuth</td><td>If sun position is not automatic,
133  this controls the horizontal angle of the sun.</td></tr>
134 <tr><td>Sun elevation</td><td>If sun position is not
135  automatic, this controls how high the sun is in the sky.</td></tr>
136 <tr><td>Moon azimuth</td><td>If moon position is not
137  automatic, this controls its horizontal angle.</td></tr>
138 <tr><td>Moon elevation</td><td>If not automatic, this controls
139  the moon's elevation</td></tr>
140 </table>
141 See the public attributes of \link simul::sky::SkyKeyframe SkyKeyframe\endlink for a full description of all the sky keyframe properties.
